abstract: Decarbonising industrial energy systems requires process-level insight into where electrification and hydrogen can replace fossil fuels. Sector-level statistics limit actionable planning. We develop a process-level allocation framework and apply it to the United Kingdom. Mapping energy across devices, end uses, and passive systems shows that electrification reduces energy demand by 24% and emissions by 39%, while hydrogen delivers 7% and 27%, respectively. A hybrid pathway matches electrification’s energy savings and achieves a 40% emissions reduction. Prioritising electrification and reserving hydrogen for residual uses provides a robust, scalable decarbonisation pathway, with a transferable framework for aligning technology deployment with net-zero targets.
